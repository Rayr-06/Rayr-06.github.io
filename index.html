05 · How I Think

Engineering philosophy

01
Domain expertise is the moat
Any developer can call a GPT API. What separates a good AI product from a generic wrapper is deep understanding of the problem domain. Six years inside real software quality work means I know exactly what breaks, why it breaks, and what a real solution needs to do.
02
Ship it. Then make it better.
Every project on this page is live and publicly accessible. Not a mockup, not a prototype. Real deployment teaches what design thinking never does — what actually breaks under real users, real data, real devices, real network conditions.
03
Honest numbers, not estimated ones
RAYR Fin shows real XIRR calculated from actual purchase data — not a percentage guess. QA Forge generates structured tests from actual requirements. The product should tell the truth, even when the truth is harder to build than a fake number.
04
Build for people, not demos
Blur mode in RAYR Fin exists because I thought about someone using their phone in public. The FD comparison exists because I know what Indian investors' first question always is. Empathy for the user is a technical requirement, not a soft skill add-on.
"Quality is not a phase at the end of development. It is a system you design from the beginning — and the best system prevents problems rather than finding them after the fact."
