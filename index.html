05 · How I Think

My Approach

Most software testing is reactive — you find bugs after they're written. I think that's the wrong place to spend engineering effort.

My goal is to build intelligent systems that catch problems before they reach users — tools that understand requirements, generate test coverage automatically, and flag anomalies through vision and AI rather than hardcoded scripts.

The same philosophy drives RAYR: instead of reacting to market moves with gut instinct, run 12 algorithms simultaneously, weigh their signals, and give traders one clear, reasoned decision. That's the standard Jim Simons used at Renaissance Technologies — and it's the standard I hold my tools to.

Quality is not a phase at the end of development. It's a system you design from the beginning.
